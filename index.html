About

I have always been fascinated by the creation of nature and engineering marvels. Many technical applications have been replicated from the principles of nature. I pursued my graduation in biomedical engineering where I understood the nature's most significant creation "THE HUMAN BODY" and engineering technologies to support human life, and I realised how these things go hand in hand in the growth of medical fraternity.

After graduating, I decided to work as a biomedical engineer in the hospital. I worked at the AIMS hospital in Ahmedabad. I was responsible for technical and engineering support during surgeries as well as other areas in the hospital. When Everything was looking smooth and straight, I witnessed some of the limitations of technology. Though medical science and technology have integrated years back and developed incredible technology and tools, there is a long way to go in innovation.

I joined IIT Gandhinagar as a summer intern to work on a research project titled "Early diagnostic and therapeutic devices for Parkinson's disease". I was appointed as a Project Assitant followed by Project Associate at IIT Gandhinagar and continued to work on the same project with Dr Vrutang Shah and Professor Harish P.M. at SysIDEA Lab. We developed a diagnostic device [PD EYE] based on pupillary light reflex to detect the onset of Parkinson's. We also developed a therapeutic tool to reduce hand tremors of Parkinsons patients.

In July 2018, I started my PhD at SysIDEA Lab. Currently, as a part of my doctoral studies, I am working on a gait trainer for stroke patients. Gait trainer will help physiotherapist to provide better rehabilitation technologies at an affordable price. I am continuing my journey as an integrator to improve the quality of life for patients.

Projects

Gait Trainer

Robotic gait trainers used in physical therapy for gait rehabilitation in humans have been an area of increased attention over the last few years.However, these devices tend to be expensive.

We are working on an alternative approach to these multiple-degree-of-freedom mechanisms are the single degree of freedom mechanisms that rely on mechanism kinematics rather than control of multiple degrees of freedom to achieve natural gait kinematics. These devices tend to be significantly cheaper while not compromising on quality of motion achievable.

Device for early diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease

Parkinson’s disease is disorder of the central nervous system that affects movement, often including tremors.

To date, the diagnosis of PD is done based on a finding of the clinical motor symptoms, a through the history of patient and response to levodopa. Further, motor symptoms appear only when approx 60% of the dopamine neurons in substantia nigra are lost.

Using control system theory approach,we are currently exploring diagnostic tool for detecting Parkinson's disease at an early stage, before the onset of other symptoms.

Prototype [PD EYE]

An inexpensive and straightforward device for early diagnosis of Parkinson's disease based on the pupillary light reflex.

Non-invasive therapeutic tool for rest tremor suppression

Resting tremor occurs when the muscle is relaxed, such as when the hands are resting on the lap.

Rest tremor is one of the most common symptoms of Parkinson's disease, and it can drastically affect the quality of life of the patients.

We are currently exploring a non-invasive therapeutic tool for rest tremor suppression. This tool can also be used to measure and characterize tremors
